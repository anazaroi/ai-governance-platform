Module Designs

Section 3 of 5 — What each module does, its key screens, and how it connects to the others.

MODULE 1

System of Record

Purpose: The authoritative inventory of every AI system in the firm. The first thing a new customer populates.

Key screens:
📋 AI Inventory table — filterable list of all models, status, tier, owner, last review date
➕ Register model form — structured intake: name, type, business unit, use case, vendor, deployment scope
🔍 Model detail page — full profile, version history, linked risk assessments, workflow history, evidence
📥 Bulk import — CSV/Excel upload for firms migrating from spreadsheets (common at launch)
Triggers → Risk Tiering Engine automatically on model registration
MODULE 2

Model & Vendor Registry

Purpose: Deeper catalogue layer — model versioning, vendor due diligence, ownership, and dependency mapping.

Key screens:
📦 Model versions list — every version of a model with change log and who approved each
🏢 Vendor directory — OpenAI, AWS Bedrock, internal teams; contract ref, country, risk status
🔗 Dependency map — which business processes depend on which models (impact analysis)
👤 Ownership matrix — model owner, business sponsor, technical owner, reviewer
Feeds → Dashboards (vendor exposure, model concentration risk)
MODULE 3

AI Risk Tiering Engine

Purpose: Structured questionnaire → weighted scoring → automated tier assignment (High / Medium / Low). Configurable per firm.

Key screens:
📝 Assessment questionnaire — 20-30 questions across 5 dimensions (see below)
📊 Scoring breakdown — dimension scores, final tier, rationale auto-generated
⚙️ Tiering config — admin can adjust weights, thresholds, add custom questions
📅 Review scheduler — auto-triggers reassessment based on tier (High = 6mo, Medium = 12mo, Low = 24mo)
5 risk dimensions: Model Complexity · Business Criticality · Data Sensitivity · Explainability · Regulatory Exposure
MODULE 4

Approval & Governance Workflow

Purpose: Structured review and sign-off for model onboarding, changes, and retirement. Powered by Inngest.

Key screens:
📬 My Tasks inbox — items awaiting your review/decision, sorted by due date
🔄 Workflow templates — configurable step sequences per workflow type and risk tier
📋 Workflow tracker — visual step-by-step progress for any model in review
✅ Decision panel — approve/reject with mandatory comments, auto-recorded to audit log
High-tier models → longer approval chains (e.g. Analyst → Model Risk → CRO). Low tier → lighter touch.
MODULE 5

Dashboards & Board Reporting

Purpose: What the CRO/COO sees. Risk portfolio at a glance, board-ready PDF reports, MAS examination readiness score.

Key screens:
🎯 CRO Dashboard — risk tier distribution, open workflows, overdue reviews, top risks
📈 Portfolio risk view — models by business unit, vendor concentration, trend over time
📄 Board report generator — one-click PDF: AI risk summary for board/BRMC meeting pack
🛡️ MAS Readiness Score — % of models assessed, % of controls evidenced, gaps flagged
This is your demo hook — show a CRO their full AI risk picture in 60 seconds.
MODULE 6

Policy & Control Library

Purpose: Pre-built MAS-mapped policies and controls. Firms select what applies, link evidence, track compliance status.

Key screens:
📚 Policy catalogue — library of MAS-mapped policies (pre-loaded, kept current by you)
🔗 Model-policy linker — assign relevant policies to each AI model by tier/use case
📁 Evidence manager — upload test results, validation docs, monitoring reports per control
🚦 Compliance heatmap — which models × controls are green/amber/red
You maintain the MAS policy templates — this is your moat. Competitors can't easily replicate current, accurate, Singapore-specific content.
